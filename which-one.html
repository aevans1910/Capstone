Which one to pick?

Sometimes it can be hard figuring out which type of animal is right for you. Although some individuals may have preferences, those may not be what is best depending on that individual's lifestyle. On this page, we will therefore discuss what type of individual is best suited to have a cat versus what individual should have a dog.

Activity

Depending on how active you are and whether or not you would like your companion to join you in that, a cat or a dog might be best.

If you are more active and want someone to join you, get a dog. If you are less active or don't want your furry companion joining you in your activities, get a cat.

Space

The amount of space you have will be a huge factor in determining what animal you can have.

If you have alot of indoor and outdoor space, a dog would absolutly love that! However, if you live in small, confined spaces, a cat would be a much better choice. If you have a large indoor space but a small outdoor space, you could get either, but if you get a dog make sure that it is a low energy dog and that you have a park nearby where it can run around.

Time

The amount of time you have available will largly determine what type of animal you should get. However, if you have no time at all or very little time, a pet might not be right for you.

If you work away from home and you are expecting your pet to be left alone for hours on end, a cat would be best. If you have more available time and are expecting to only leave your pet alone for a few hours at most, then you may get a dog. Just make sure that the dog doesn't have seperation anxiety!
